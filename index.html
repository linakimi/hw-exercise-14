Maine Coon

Fluff no.1

Large, fluffy cat breeds include the Maine Coon, Ragdoll, Persian, Siberian, and Norwegian Forest Cat, all known for bushy tails and thick coats that usually need grooming. Other popular plush breeds are the Birman, Himalayan, and British Longhair, with some, like Ragdolls and Birmans, being easier to maintain.

Norwegian Forest Cat

Fluff no.2

The Norwegian Forest Cat is a large, sturdy breed known for its thick, water-resistant double coat, bushy tail, and tufted ears. Originating in Norway, it’s well adapted to cold climates and is intelligent, gentle, and independent, yet affectionate with its family.

Ragdoll

Fluff no.3

The Ragdoll is a large, gentle cat known for its striking blue eyes, soft semi-long coat, and relaxed, affectionate nature. It is very people-oriented, often going limp when held, and has fur that is plush but relatively easy to maintain.

Birman

Fluff no.4

The Birman is a medium-to-large, affectionate cat known for its silky, semi-long coat, striking blue eyes, and distinctive white “gloves” on its paws. It has a gentle, social temperament and a coat that is less prone to matting than many longhaired breeds.

Persian

Fluff no.5

The Persian is a calm, elegant cat known for its long, dense coat, flat face, and large round eyes. It has a gentle, laid-back personality but requires daily grooming to keep its fur healthy and tangle-free.

Siberian

Fluff no.6

The Siberian cat is a large, muscular breed with a thick triple coat, bushy tail, and strong build, developed to withstand harsh Russian winters. It is intelligent, playful, and affectionate, and its fur is surprisingly low-shedding and resistant to matting despite its fullness.
